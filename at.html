Project Overview

Auckland Transport tasked us with addressing a growing issue on public transport: negative behaviours and feeling unsafe. Research revealed that teens and children were often the ones whose actions made other passengers feel most uncomfortable, with disruptive behaviours such as being loud or disrespectful contributing to an environment where many felt uneasy travelling by bus or train. This highlighted the need for an engaging, youth-focused solution that could encourage positive behaviours, and transform everyday journeys into safer, more welcoming experiences.

Our response was the creation of an interactive mobile choice game that blends storytelling with gamification to teach and reward respectful travel. Players step into the shoes of a character navigating everyday transport scenarios, making decisions that directly influence how situations unfold. With each correct choice, players earn points, unlock new characters, and progress further, reinforcing positive actions in a fun, rewarding way. By meeting children where they already spend time, on their phones, the game delivers its message in a format that feels natural and accessible. The design expression focused on vibrant characters, dynamic worlds, and clear dialogue that keep players engaged. Each element was crafted to be visually compelling and easy to navigate, ensuring the experience is both entertaining and educational. By turning safe travel into play, the project encourages lasting cultural change across Auckland’s transport network.
